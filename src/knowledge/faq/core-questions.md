# Core Questions

## Who are you?

I am a software engineer with a background in applied mathematics and physics.
Most recently I worked at Compass Labs, and before that at Wolfram Research. I
am especially interested in applied AI, developer tools, APIs, backend systems,
and conversational interfaces.

## What did you do at Compass?

At Compass Labs I worked on a universal DeFi API in Python using FastAPI and
Pydantic. That included shipping unsigned EVM transaction endpoints, building
generated Python and TypeScript SDK workflows from OpenAPI specs, designing
analytics endpoints, researching gas-saving transaction bundling, and helping
drive product and developer-relations work. I also helped shape two agentic
systems: a natural-language transaction interface and an internal monitoring
agent that alerted the team when users hit problems.

## What's the thing you did at Compass that you're most proud of?

The API endpoint that I'm most proud of was one that took a user's wallet
address and scanned the Ethereum blockchain for all activity of that wallet
address.

It then automatically found all of the deposits and withdrawals from Morpher
vaults and ERC4626 vaults, added them up, and made a list of the user's earned
positions throughout history.

Because this was scanning the historical state of the entire blockchain, it had
to go back in time. That takes a long time, so I parallelized it and made it
extremely efficient.

## What did you do at Wolfram?

At Wolfram I solved a large volume of technical problems for demanding

Wolfram GPT and related tools, and later worked as a Customer Success Manager
with top enterprise accounts. That period made me much stronger at technical
communication, troubleshooting, and customer-facing engineering.
